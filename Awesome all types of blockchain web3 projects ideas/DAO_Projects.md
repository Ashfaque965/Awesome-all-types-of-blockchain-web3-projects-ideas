# What is a DAO?

A **Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO)** is an organization whose rules and governance are enforced by smart contracts instead of centralized management.

Members participate through governance tokens or other voting mechanisms to make decisions collectively.

---

# Key Characteristics

* Decentralized Governance
* Transparent Decision Making
* Community Ownership
* Smart Contract Automation
* Treasury Management
* On-chain Voting
* Permissionless Participation (depending on the DAO)
* Immutable Governance History

# DAO Governance Models

## Token-Based Governance

Voting power depends on token ownership.

---

## One Person, One Vote

Identity-based governance.

---

## Quadratic Voting

Voting cost increases quadratically, reducing dominance by large holders.

---

## Reputation-Based Governance

Voting power depends on reputation rather than token balances.

---

## Delegated Governance

Members delegate voting rights to trusted representatives.

---

## Multi-Signature Governance

Critical actions require approval from multiple authorized members.
