A consortium blockchain (also called a federated blockchain) is governed by a group of trusted organizations rather than a single company (private blockchain) or anyone (public blockchain). It is ideal for industries where multiple organizations need to share data and processes while maintaining privacy and collective governance.

When to Use a Consortium Blockchain

A consortium blockchain is the best choice when:

Multiple organizations need to collaborate without a single party controlling the network.
Participants are known and permissioned.
Privacy and regulatory compliance are important.
Transactions require shared trust and auditability.
Governance decisions are made collectively.

Common examples include banking consortia, healthcare provider networks, international supply chains, university credential networks, government agency collaboration, and industry-wide logistics ecosystems.

Public vs Private vs Hybrid vs Consortium
Type	Control	Participants	Transparency	Typical Use Cases
Public	Anyone	Open	Fully public	Cryptocurrencies, public dApps
Private	One organization	Permissioned	Private	Internal enterprise systems
Hybrid	One or more organizations + public chain	Mixed	Selective	Enterprises requiring both privacy and public verification
Consortium	Multiple organizations	Permissioned	Shared among members	Banking, healthcare, supply chains, government collaboration

Consortium blockchains are particularly effective when multiple independent organizations need a shared, trusted system without handing control to a single entity. They are widely adopted in sectors such as finance, healthcare, logistics, manufacturing, energy, and government, where collaboration and shared governance are essential.
